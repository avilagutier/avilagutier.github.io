I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Engineering at the University of Messina, specializing in Analytical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, and Materials Science. My career has been dedicated to the design and synthesis of innovative (nano)materials, with a focus on their characterization and data-driven problem-solving.

My passion for materials science began with my Bachelor's degree in Chemistry from the University of Guanajuato, which I built upon with a Master's from the École Nationale Supérieure de Chimie de Paris. I then earned my Ph.D. in Physical and Analytical Chemistry at Sorbonne Université, conducting research with the "MONARIS - CNRS" group.

I've since gained diverse postdoctoral experience across Mexico, the United States, and Italy, where I've contributed to projects developing cutting-edge photoluminescent materials, carbon materials, and composite energy storage solutions.
