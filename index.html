I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Engineering at the University of Messina, specializing in Analytical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, and the design of advanced Materials for Thermal Energy Storage (TES). My research focuses on the synthesis of innovative (nano)materials, leveraging advanced characterization techniques and data-driven approaches to solve complex energy and environmental challenges.

My academic journey began with a B.Sc. in Chemistry from the University of Guanajuato. I further specialized with a Master’s degree from Chimie ParisTech (PSL University) and earned my Ph.D. in Physical and Analytical Chemistry at Sorbonne Université, where I conducted research within the MONARIS-CNRS group.

Since then, I have built a diverse international career with postdoctoral stays in Mexico, the United States, and Italy. My work has contributed to cutting-edge projects involving photoluminescent zeolites, carbon-based nanomaterials, and composite solutions for efficient energy storage.
